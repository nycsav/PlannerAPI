# PlannerAPI Editorial Voice & Tone

## 1. Audience

- CMOs and VPs of Marketing
- Agency owners and strategists
- Brand, performance, and CX leads

Assume:
- High context on marketing and AI
- Low patience for fluff
- Need to translate noise into budget, roadmap, and org decisions

## 3. Tone Examples

### Good

- "McKinsey's 2026 survey shows only 6% of marketing orgs reach AI maturity, but those teams see 22% efficiency gains. The gap between leaders and laggards is widening."
- "Google's AI Overviews now cover 40% of commercial queries in retail. If you sell through search, this quietly shrinks the real estate you control."
- "Your next move: audit the top 25 queries that drive paid search revenue and see how many trigger an AI Overview."

### Not Good

- "AI is transforming everything and marketing leaders must adapt fast or be left behind."
- "You should consider AI when thinking about your content strategy."
- "This is a game-changer for everyone."

## 6. Do / Don't Reference

### Do

- Write as if briefing a busy CMO before a board meeting
- Use present tense when describing implications ("this puts pressure onâ€¦")
- Quantify whenever possible ("22% efficiency gains", "40% coverage")
- Name specific roles affected ("For mid-market agency ownersâ€¦")
- Make every card citable in a board presentation

### Don't

- Use emojis
- Use hype phrases
- Re-explain basic marketing concepts (assume literacy)
- Write vague recommendations ("consider evaluating")
- Produce content that could appear in any generic AI newsletter

## 7. Differentiation Tests

Before publishing, every piece should pass:

1. **The "So What?" Test:** Can a CMO act on this in 10 seconds?
2. **The "Next Move" Test:** Is there one clear, operator-ready action a CMO can take next?
3. **The "Citation Test:** Can this insight be quoted in a board presentation?
4. **The "Differentiation Test:** Would this appear in a generic AI news aggregator? (If yes, revise.)

## 8. Application in Daily Intelligence

When generating or editing Daily Intelligence cards:

- Ensure the **title** uses a tension pattern when possible
- Ensure the **summary** ends with "what this means" language for the relevant pillar
- Ensure **moves[0]** starts with "Your next move:" and is specific

Example move:
> "Your next move: Ask your analytics lead to quantify how many of your top 50 search queries now show AI Overviews, and which competitors' content is being lifted into those boxes."
